# KYC Process, KRAs and Record Lifecycle

How an individual's KYC record is created, validated, shared and updated in
the Indian securities market.

## What is a KRA?

A **KYC Registration Agency (KRA)** is a SEBI-registered body that
centralises KYC records so an investor completes KYC only once. The five
KRAs are CVL KRA (CDSL Ventures Limited), NDML KRA, DOTEX KRA (NSE), CAMS
KRA and Karvy KRA. All KRAs are interoperable — a record registered with one
is retrievable by intermediaries through any of them.

## The KYC process, step by step

1. **Fill the KYC form** — the individual KYC application form with identity
   and address details (see the CVL KYC Form Guide).
2. **Attach documents** — self-attested PAN copy, one Officially Valid
   Document for identity, address proof, and a signed photograph.
3. **In-Person Verification (IPV)** — an authorised person of a
   SEBI-registered intermediary verifies the applicant and the original
   documents (video IPV is permitted for online onboarding).
4. **Submission to the KRA** — the intermediary uploads the form and
   documents to a KRA within the prescribed timelines.
5. **Validation** — the KRA independently validates the record: PAN is
   checked against the Income Tax database, and the mobile number and email
   address are verified with one-time validation links/messages.
6. **Status assignment** — the record receives a KYC status that every
   intermediary can query using the investor's PAN.

## KYC statuses

- **KYC Validated**: all attributes (PAN, mobile, email) verified by the
  KRA. The investor can transact with ANY intermediary without redoing KYC.
- **KYC Registered / Verified**: the record exists and documents are in
  order, but not every attribute is independently validated. The investor
  can continue with existing intermediaries; a new intermediary may ask for
  re-confirmation of details.
- **KYC On-Hold**: a validation attribute failed (e.g. mobile/email link not
  confirmed, PAN not linked as required). Transactions may be blocked until
  the deficiency is resolved.
- **KYC Rejected**: the record failed verification (e.g. document mismatch);
  fresh KYC with correct documents is required.

An investor can check their KYC status on any KRA website (for example,
www.cvlkra.com) by entering their PAN.

## Updating a KYC record (KYC modification)

To change address, mobile number, email, name or other attributes, the
investor submits a **KYC Details Change form** with proof of the changed
attribute to any SEBI-registered intermediary. The update flows through the
KRA to every intermediary the investor deals with — there is no need to
update each one separately. Address changes require a fresh proof of
address; name changes require supporting documents such as a gazette
notification, marriage certificate, or updated PAN card.

## Timelines and record-keeping

- Intermediaries must upload KYC records to the KRA promptly after account
  opening (within the timelines prescribed by SEBI regulations).
- Original KYC documents remain with the intermediary that performed the
  KYC; the KRA holds scanned copies and the validation status.
- KYC records are retained for at least the periods mandated under the PMLA
  and SEBI regulations, including after the business relationship ends.

## Why KYC can block a transaction

Common reasons an existing investor is asked to redo or remediate KYC:

- The record is On-Hold because the mobile number or email was never
  validated.
- PAN-Aadhaar linkage requirements were not met, making the PAN inoperative.
- The KYC was done years ago with a non-OVD proof that is no longer
  accepted, so the record cannot be upgraded to Validated.
- A mismatch between the name on the PAN and the name in the KYC record.
