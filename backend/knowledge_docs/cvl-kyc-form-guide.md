# CVL KYC Individual Form Guide

Official field-by-field guidance for the CVL (CDSL Ventures Limited) KYC
Application Form for Individuals, as used across SEBI-registered
intermediaries (mutual funds, brokers, depository participants).

## Purpose of the form

The CVL Individual KYC form captures the identity and address details of an
individual investor once, records them with a KYC Registration Agency (KRA),
and lets every SEBI-registered intermediary reuse the verified record. An
investor who is already KYC-registered does not need to repeat the full
process when opening an account with a new intermediary.

## Identity Details section

- **Name**: must be written exactly as it appears on the PAN card. Expansions
  or abbreviations that differ from the PAN record cause rejection at the KRA.
- **Father's / Spouse's name**: either is acceptable; married women may give
  the spouse's name instead of the father's name.
- **PAN (Permanent Account Number)**: mandatory for investing in the Indian
  securities market. The PAN is the sole identification number for the KYC
  record, and a self-attested copy of the PAN card must be attached. The PAN
  format is five letters, four digits, one letter (e.g. ABCDE1234F).
- **Date of birth**: as per the proof submitted; it must match the PAN record.
- **Gender, Marital status, Nationality**: tick-box selections. Non-resident
  applicants must additionally provide their overseas address.
- **Status**: Resident Individual, Non-Resident Indian (NRI), or Foreign
  National. NRIs must submit a certified copy of the passport and, where
  applicable, the PIO/OCI card.
- **Photograph**: a recent passport-size photograph must be affixed and
  signed across.

## Address Details section

- **Correspondence address**: where the intermediary sends statements and
  notices. Proof is required (see the accepted proofs reference document).
- **Permanent address**: required when it differs from the correspondence
  address; NRIs must give their overseas permanent address.
- **Contact details**: telephone and mobile numbers with country/STD code,
  and the email address used for electronic statements. Mobile number and
  email are used by the KRA for record-validation messages.

## Other Details section

- **Gross annual income**: select the income slab (below 1 lakh, 1-5 lakh,
  5-10 lakh, 10-25 lakh, above 25 lakh) OR give the net worth with its date.
  This information must be kept updated with the intermediary.
- **Occupation**: private sector, public sector, government service,
  business, professional, agriculturist, retired, housewife, student, others.
- **Politically Exposed Person (PEP)**: the applicant must declare whether
  they are a PEP, related to a PEP, or neither. PEPs are individuals
  entrusted with prominent public functions in a foreign country.

## Declaration and signature

The applicant declares that the details are true and correct, and undertakes
to inform the intermediary of any change forthwith. The form is signed and
dated; for non-individual signatories, the authorised signatory signs with
the entity stamp. Providing false information can lead to action under the
Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002.

## In-Person Verification (IPV)

A SEBI-registered intermediary must conduct in-person verification of the
applicant: an authorised official checks the original documents against the
copies and records their name, designation, organisation, signature and the
IPV date on the form. IPV performed by one SEBI-registered intermediary is
valid for all others; for online account opening, IPV may be performed over
video in accordance with SEBI circulars.

## Common rejection reasons

- Name, date of birth, or father's name not matching the PAN record.
- Missing self-attestation on document copies, or missing original-seen
  verification (OSV) stamp.
- Address proof older than permitted, expired, or in a third party's name.
- Photograph missing or not signed across.
- Overwriting or corrections on the form without counter-signature.
