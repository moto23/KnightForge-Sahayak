# Accepted Identity and Address Proofs for KYC

Reference list of documents accepted as proof of identity and proof of
address for individual KYC under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act
(PMLA) rules and SEBI's KYC requirements.

## Officially Valid Documents (OVDs)

The following six documents are Officially Valid Documents. Any one OVD
serves as proof of identity, and — when it carries the applicant's current
address — also as proof of address:

1. **Passport** (must be within its validity period)
2. **Driving licence** (must be within its validity period)
3. **Proof of possession of Aadhaar number** (with the Aadhaar number
   suitably masked where required)
4. **Voter's Identity Card** issued by the Election Commission of India
5. **Job card issued by NREGA**, duly signed by an officer of the State
   Government
6. **Letter issued by the National Population Register** containing name and
   address details

## Proof of Identity

- The **PAN card is mandatory** for securities-market KYC and is always
  collected in addition to an OVD; it establishes identity but NOT address.
- The identity proof copy must be **self-attested** by the applicant, and the
  original must be shown for verification (originals seen and verified).
- The name on the proof of identity must match the name on the KYC form and
  the PAN record.

## Proof of Address

- Any OVD containing the applicant's current address is acceptable.
- If the OVD does not carry the current address, the following are accepted
  as **deemed OVDs** for a limited period (a current-address OVD must then be
  submitted within three months):
  - Utility bill (electricity, telephone, post-paid mobile, piped gas,
    water) **not more than two months old**
  - Property or municipal tax receipt
  - Pension or family pension payment orders issued by a government
    department or PSU, if they contain the address
  - Letter of allotment of accommodation from a government employer, or a
    leave-and-licence agreement with such an employer allotting official
    accommodation
- The address proof must be in the applicant's own name. Documents in the
  name of a spouse or parent are not accepted for regular individual KYC.
- If the correspondence address and permanent address differ, proof is
  required for both.

## Documents for special categories

- **NRIs**: certified copy of the passport is mandatory; overseas address
  proof is required, and copies attested abroad must be attested by
  authorised officials such as the Indian embassy or consulate, a notary
  public, or a scheduled commercial bank branch overseas.
- **Foreign nationals / PIO / OCI**: passport plus PIO/OCI card where
  applicable, with overseas address proof.
- **Minors**: the guardian completes KYC with the guardian's documents,
  along with the minor's proof of date of birth (e.g. birth certificate).

## Attestation rules

- Every copy must be **self-attested** (signed by the applicant across the
  copy) and verified against the original by the intermediary's authorised
  person, who stamps it "Originals Seen and Verified" with name, designation
  and signature.
- Expired documents (lapsed passport or driving licence) are not valid
  proofs.
- Laminated or damaged documents where details are unreadable may be
  rejected.
